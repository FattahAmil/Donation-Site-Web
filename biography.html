Pablo Picasso

Artist

Here's a time line of Pablo Picasso :

1881: Born in Málaga, Spain - Pablo Diego José Francisco de Paula Juan Nepomuceno María de los Remedios Cipriano de la Santísima Trinidad Martyr Patricio Clito Ruíz y Picasso (whew!) was born on October 25, 1881.

1895: First Exhibition - Picasso's talent was evident at an early age. He held his first exhibition in Barcelona at the age of 14.

1901-1904: Blue Period - Picasso's work during this period was characterized by a predominantly blue palette and themes of poverty, isolation, and despair.

1905-1906: Rose Period - Transitioning from the Blue Period, Picasso's work took on warmer tones and subject matter, often featuring circus performers and harlequins.

1907: Les Demoiselles d'Avignon - This groundbreaking painting marked the beginning of Cubism, an art movement that Picasso co-founded with Georges Braque.

1917: Collaboration with Jean Cocteau - Picasso collaborated with the French poet and artist Jean Cocteau on the ballet "Parade."

1960s: Late Period - Picasso continued to create art prolifically until his death, experimenting with different styles and techniques.

1973: Death - Picasso passed away on April 8, 1973, in Mougins, France, at the age of 91.
